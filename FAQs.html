    FAQ's

    Frequently Asked Questions

    What kind of academic support resources are available to me as a Fisher College student, and how can I access them?

    Academic support resources available at Fisher College can include tutoring services, academic advising, and the Writing Center. They can be accessed through the school's website or by contacting the relevant department.

    How can I get involved in clubs and organizations on campus, and what kind of options are available?

    To get involved in clubs and organizations at Fisher College, students can attend club fairs, visit the Student Activities Office, or check out the school's website. Options can include academic, cultural, athletic, and community service organizations.

    What are some important campus policies and procedures that I should be aware of?

    Important campus policies and procedures to be aware of at Fisher College can include those related to academic integrity, sexual misconduct, and student conduct. These can be found in the school's student handbook or by contacting the appropriate department.

    How can I connect with other students and build a community at Fisher College?

    Students can connect with others and build a community at Fisher College by attending campus events, joining clubs or organizations, participating in intramural sports, or living in a residence hall.

    What kind of campus services are available to me as a student, such as health services or career counseling?

    Campus services available to students at Fisher College can include health services, counseling and psychological services, career services, and disability services. Information on these can be found on the school's website or by contacting the relevant department.

    How can I make the most of my college experience at Fisher College, both academically and socially?

    To make the most of their college experience at Fisher College, students can prioritize their academics, get involved in campus life, and take advantage of available resources and opportunities.

    What are some recommended study and time management strategies for college students?

    Recommended study and time management strategies for college students can include creating a study schedule, breaking up studying into manageable chunks, and minimizing distractions. Time management strategies can include prioritizing tasks and setting achievable goals.

    How can I balance my academic responsibilities with my social life and other commitments?

    Balancing academic responsibilities with social life and other commitments can be achieved through effective time management and prioritization, as well as setting boundaries and communicating with others.

    What are some recommended resources and tips for navigating the transition to college life at Fisher College?

    Recommended resources and tips for navigating the transition to college life at Fisher College can include attending orientation events, seeking out academic and social support resources, and taking advantage of available opportunities.

    What are some ways that I can get involved in the local community and give back while I am a student at Fisher College?

    To get involved in the local community and give back while a student at Fisher College, students can participate in community service events and programs, internships, or volunteer opportunities through clubs or organizations.
